# 30-Day Launch Plan — First Paying Pilot Client

**Revora AI Front Desk Team / Mercy AI Staff**  
*Mercy Speaks Digital*

A simple, action-first roadmap to turn your **working demo** into your **first paying pilot** in 30 days. Follow the weeks in order, use the daily checklist, and track your numbers. Adjust as you learn—speed of learning beats perfection.

---

## 1. Goal

**Goal:** Get the **first paying pilot client** for **Revora AI Front Desk Team / Mercy AI Staff** within **30 days**.

“First client” here means **money collected** (setup fee and/or first month) and a **clear pilot agreement**—not a vague “maybe later.”

## 3. Target niche for first 30 days

- High missed-call cost, clear front-desk pain, familiar with software spend, strong local Maps presence.

**Secondary (after Week 2 if dental is slow, or in parallel with a separate list):**

Pick **one primary niche** for your main list so your messages stay tight. Add secondary niches only if you can keep lists and scripts separate without diluting focus.

| Task | What “done” looks like |
|------|-------------------------|

| **Practice the 10-minute demo** | You finish in ~10 minutes, every time, using `10_MINUTE_DEMO_SCRIPT.md` (or the polished PDF). |

| **Create simple tracking sheet** | Columns for: business, channel, date contacted, follow-up date, reply (Y/N), demo booked, outcome. Spreadsheet is enough. |

## 5. Week 2: Outreach

**Cadence:** **Contact 10 businesses per day** (50 in Week 2 if you hit 5 days × 10; adjust if you started mid-week).

| Task | Notes |
|------|--------|
| **Use Google Maps, Facebook, Instagram, and websites** | Maps for discovery; social and sites for DMs, contact forms, or “info@” emails. |
| **Send short cold email/DM** | One clear problem + one outcome + **ask for a 10-minute demo**—no essay. |
| **Offer a 10-minute demo** | Calendly or “reply with two times”—reduce friction. |
| **Track replies** | Same sheet as Week 1; color-code “interested” vs “not now.” |
| **Follow up after 2 days** | Polite bump; most deals need 2–3 touches before a reply. |

If someone asks “Is it live for everyone?” answer honestly using Section 10 language.

## 6. Week 3: Demo and follow-up

| Task | Notes |
|------|--------|
| **Run demos for interested businesses** | Same structure every time; leave 5 minutes for questions. |
| **Use the One Page Offer Sheet** | Screen-share or send PDF after—anchors price and scope. |
| **Walk through the Demo Hub** | Tie each screen to *their* missed calls, chaos, or follow-up gaps. |
| **Ask business pain-point questions** | “What happens when the phone rings and you’re with a client?” “How do you follow up today?” |
| **Send Client Setup Form after interest** | Only when they’re warm—shows you’re serious and filters tire-kickers. |
| **Send Proposal Template if they want pricing** | Customize dollar amounts and pilot scope; keep it **pilot/onboarding** framed. |

## 7. Week 4: Close first pilot

| Task | Notes |
|------|--------|
| **Offer a pilot package** | Named “pilot” or “onboarding setup”—not “enterprise rollout day one.” |
| **Collect setup fee** | Invoice before heavy configuration; aligns incentives. |
| **Complete Client Setup Form** | No build without answers; use `CLIENT_ONBOARDING_CHECKLIST.md` internally if helpful. |
| **Prepare client-specific configuration** | Scripts, FAQs, hours, tone—document what you changed. |
| **Do not promise full automation until production database wiring is complete** | Be explicit: **production automation** comes **after onboarding** and **final database/integration setup**. |
| **Position it as a pilot/onboarding setup** | They get real value and a **working demo** path to production—you get revenue and a case study. |

## 8. Daily action checklist

Use this every weekday (and optionally one weekend block):

- [ ] **Review demo for 10 minutes** — one full pass or one weak section until it’s smooth.  
- [ ] **Contact 10 businesses** — new outbound; mix channels if needed.  
- [ ] **Follow up with previous contacts** — anyone past the 2-day mark with no reply.  
- [ ] **Improve one sales message** — tweak subject line, first line, or CTA based on yesterday’s replies.  
- [ ] **Track all responses** — update the sheet before you log off.  

If you miss a day, **do not** try to “catch up” with 30 contacts tomorrow—return to 10 + follow-ups so you stay consistent.

## 9. Pilot offer recommendation

Present both; **default to one** so you sound confident.

### Starter Pilot

- **$497** setup  
- **$197**/month  
- **Includes:** AI lead capture + qualification + follow-up drafts + dashboard demo experience aligned to their business  

### Pro Pilot

- **$997** setup  
- **$397**/month  
- **Adds:** booking intent, handoff workflow, missed revenue tracking (as surfaced in your demo story), and marketing/social drafts where appropriate  

Adjust numbers only if you have a clear reason (market, geography, or scope). Write the scope in the proposal—**pilot-ready**, **client-specific setup**, timeline for moving toward **production automation after onboarding and final database/integration setup**.

---

## 10. What to say honestly

**Use language like:**

- **“Working demo”** — real flows you can show today.  
- **“Pilot-ready”** — appropriate for a first paid engagement with clear boundaries.  
- **“Client-specific setup”** — you configure for their business, not a generic widget.  
- **“Production automation after onboarding and final database/integration setup”** — sets correct expectations for full hands-off operation tied to their stack.  

**Do not say:**

- “Fully autonomous” (implies zero oversight and unlimited liability)  
- “Guaranteed revenue” (you cannot promise their market or operations)  
- “Fully live production system” (unless every integration and database path is truly live for them—verify before claiming)  
- “No human needed” (undermines trust and ignores compliance, exceptions, and quality control)  

You are selling **speed, consistency, and a professional front desk experience** on a **pilot**—not magic.

## 11. Success metrics

Track weekly (minimum) in your sheet:

| Metric | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Businesses contacted** | Volume × learning; proves you showed up. |
| **Replies** | Message-market fit signal. |
| **Demos booked** | Pipeline health. |
| **Demos completed** | Skill and offer clarity. |
| **Proposals sent** | Serious buyers identified. |
| **Clients closed** | The only metric that pays rent. |

A good Week 2–3 benchmark for a beginner: **50+ contacts**, **5–15 replies**, **2–5 demos booked**. Closing one pilot in 30 days is a win even if the numbers are modest at first.

---

## 12. End-of-30-days review

Answer these in writing (15 minutes):

1. **Which niche responded best?** (Dental vs secondary lists.)  
2. **Which message worked best?** (Subject, hook, channel.)  
3. **Which objection came up most?** (Price, trust, “AI,” timing—document exact words.)  
4. **What needs to be improved?** (Demo, offer, speed, assets.)  
5. **Did we close a pilot?** (If yes: what worked? If no: what was the closest loss?)  
6. **What is next month’s focus?** (Double down on winning niche, rewrite one script, or tighten pilot scope.)
