# Client Onboarding Checklist

**Revora AI Front Desk Team / Mercy AI Staff**
*Powered by Mercy Speaks Digital*

Internal checklist for taking a new client from "signed" to "AI employee live." Use this every time. Don't skip steps — the order is the order for a reason.

> **Internal use.** This is the team-side checklist. The client-facing version is `CLIENT_SETUP_FORM.md`.

---

## Phase 0 — Pre-Kickoff (Day 0)

Goal: have everything ready before the first call so kickoff feels effortless.

- [ ] Contract signed and countersigned
- [ ] Deposit / first invoice paid
- [ ] Client added to internal CRM with tier (Starter / Pro / Premium)
- [ ] `CLIENT_SETUP_FORM.md` sent to the client with a 48-hour soft deadline
- [ ] Kickoff call scheduled (30 minutes)
- [ ] Internal project folder created (named: `Client - [Business Name]`)
- [ ] Account manager assigned
- [ ] Build lead assigned

---

## Phase 1 — Kickoff Call (Day 1)

Goal: confirm scope, confirm goals, fill in any blanks from the setup form.

- [ ] Setup form reviewed line by line with the client
- [ ] #1 priority confirmed in writing (missed calls, bookings, follow-up, etc.)
- [ ] Business hours confirmed
- [ ] Top 5 services confirmed with prices (if AI is allowed to quote)
- [ ] Escalation rules confirmed (who gets called, when)
- [ ] Tone preference confirmed
- [ ] Approval rules confirmed
- [ ] Launch target date agreed (typical: 7–14 days)
- [ ] Recap email sent within 2 hours of the call

---

## Phase 2 — Discovery & Content (Days 2–4)

Goal: collect every piece of business knowledge the AI will need.

- [ ] FAQs collected (target: 10 minimum)
- [ ] Service descriptions written and approved
- [ ] Pricing rules documented (what AI can quote, what it can't)
- [ ] Booking link confirmed and tested
- [ ] Booking flow walked through manually by our team
- [ ] Owner / team contact list confirmed
- [ ] Sample customer calls / DMs reviewed (if available)
- [ ] Brand voice samples collected (website, social, past emails)
- [ ] Words / phrases to avoid documented

---

## Phase 3 — AI Build (Days 4–8)

Goal: configure the AI employee so it sounds, behaves, and books like the client.

- [ ] AI agent created and named in workspace
- [ ] Tone settings applied
- [ ] FAQs loaded
- [ ] Services + pricing rules loaded
- [ ] Booking flow configured
- [ ] Escalation rules configured
- [ ] Approval rules configured
- [ ] Internal voice / chat test pass #1 completed
- [ ] Internal voice / chat test pass #2 completed
- [ ] Edge-case tests run (angry caller, pricing push, emergency, after-hours)
- [ ] Build lead signs off internally before client preview

---

## Phase 4 — Client Preview & Approval (Days 8–10)

Goal: get the client to hear / see the AI before launch and approve it.

- [ ] Preview call scheduled with client
- [ ] Live preview demo delivered
- [ ] Client given a test phone number / chat to try it themselves
- [ ] Client feedback captured in writing
- [ ] Adjustments applied
- [ ] Final client sign-off received (email or signature)

---

## Phase 5 — Launch (Days 10–14)

Goal: route real customers to the AI employee and watch the first 72 hours closely.

- [ ] Phone number / chat / DM channels connected
- [ ] Routing rules live (which calls go to AI vs. human)
- [ ] After-hours behavior verified
- [ ] First 24-hour monitoring window assigned to build lead
- [ ] First 72-hour monitoring window assigned to account manager
- [ ] Day-1 summary email sent to client
- [ ] Day-3 check-in call scheduled

---

## Phase 6 — First 30 Days (Days 14–44)

Goal: prove ROI, lock in trust, and earn the renewal.

- [ ] Week 1 performance report sent
- [ ] Week 2 performance report sent
- [ ] Week 3 performance report sent
- [ ] 30-day review call held
- [ ] Missed-revenue dollars captured documented
- [ ] Client testimonial requested (if results are strong)
- [ ] Upsell conversation (marketing AI lane, additional locations, etc.)
- [ ] Renewal / next-month invoice confirmed

---

## Definition of Done

A client is considered fully onboarded when **all** of these are true:

- [ ] AI employee is live on the client's real channels
- [ ] Client has signed off on tone, scope, and approval rules
- [ ] At least one real customer interaction has been reviewed and is healthy
- [ ] 30-day review call is on the calendar
- [ ] All onboarding documents are stored in the client's internal project folder

---

## Red Flags — Pause Onboarding If You See These

Stop and escalate to the team lead before continuing if any of these happen:

- Client cannot decide on tone or scope after two calls
- Client refuses to provide FAQs or service pricing
- Client wants AI to do something we don't currently support (don't promise — log it)
- Booking link or calendar access keeps failing
- Client misses two scheduled calls in a row

A paused onboarding is always cheaper than a bad launch.
