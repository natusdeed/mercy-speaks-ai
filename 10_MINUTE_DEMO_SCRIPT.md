# 10-Minute Demo Script

**Revora AI Front Desk Team / Mercy AI Staff**
*Powered by Mercy Speaks Digital*

A tight, repeatable 10-minute live demo. Built so any team member can run it the same way and land the same close. Time yourself the first three runs — the magic is in the pacing.

---

## Before You Start

- [ ] Browser open to `/ai-employee-system`
- [ ] All `/demo/*` tabs pre-loaded in order (Hub → Command Center → AI Employees → Lead Ops → Missed Revenue → Approvals → Marketing/Social)
- [ ] Your camera on, mic tested, distractions closed
- [ ] One-sentence pitch memorized (see Section 0)
- [ ] Calendar link ready to paste at the close

---

## Section 0 — Anchor Sentence (memorize this)

> "A 24/7 AI employee team that captures leads, qualifies them, prepares booking and follow-up, flags missed revenue, and helps create marketing content."

Use it whenever the prospect drifts. Always come back to outcomes.

---

## Minute 0:00 — 0:45 — Welcome & Frame

> "Thanks for jumping on. I'm going to keep this tight — 10 minutes, no slides. I'll show you exactly what our AI Front Desk does, using a business like yours as the example. At the end, you'll know if it's a fit or not. Fair?"

**Goal:** earn permission to drive.

---

## Minute 0:45 — 2:00 — The Problem (Their Reality)

Pick the one most relevant to their niche:

- "Most [niche] we work with miss 30–50% of inbound calls because the owner is on the chair / on a job / with a patient."
- "Every missed call is a booked appointment your competitor catches."
- "And after-hours? Voicemails almost never convert."

> "So the question we kept hearing was: *what if you had a front desk that never slept and never missed?* That's what we built."

**Open `/ai-employee-system`.**

> "This is the public-facing page so people can see what it is. But the real magic is inside. Let me show you."

---

## Minute 2:00 — 3:00 — The Hub

**Open `/demo`.**

> "This is the AI Employee hub. Every box on this screen is a different AI employee with a specific job — receptionist, qualifier, recovery, marketing. Think of it like a small team that runs 24/7 and never calls in sick."

Hover over one or two cards. Don't over-explain.

---

## Minute 3:00 — 4:30 — Command Center

**Open `/demo/command-center`.**

> "This is what you'd open every morning with coffee. Calls handled, leads captured, revenue saved — at a glance. You don't have to log into five tools. One screen tells you what your AI team did overnight."

Point at the dollar figures.

> "Notice the *Revenue Saved* number. That's calls and leads that would normally die. The AI caught them, qualified them, and booked or flagged them while you slept."

---

## Minute 4:30 — 5:30 — AI Employees

**Open `/demo/ai-employees`.**

> "Here's the roster. Each AI employee owns a lane — like real staff. Receptionist picks up calls. Qualifier asks the right questions. Recovery chases the no-shows and dormant leads. Marketing drafts your social and review replies."

> "You don't have to hire four people. You hire one system that does the job of four."

---

## Minute 5:30 — 6:30 — Lead Ops (The Pipeline)

**Open `/demo/lead-ops`.**

> "This is how a new lead actually moves through the system. First touch, qualification, then booked. You don't have to babysit any of it — you just see the result."

> "If a lead stalls, the AI follows up. If they go cold, recovery picks them up. Every lead has a clear status."

---

## Minute 6:30 — 7:30 — Missed Revenue (The Wow Moment)

**Open `/demo/missed-revenue`.**

This is the page that closes deals. Slow down here.

> "This is the part owners love. Every call you missed yesterday, every no-show, every dormant lead — with the dollar value attached."

> "Most [niche] are walking past 5–15 booked appointments a month and don't even know it. This page tells you exactly how much you left on the table — and then the AI goes and tries to recover it."

Pause. Let them absorb the number.

---

## Minute 7:30 — 8:15 — Approvals (Trust)

**Open `/demo/approvals`.**

> "Now here's the part that makes owners comfortable. Nothing goes out without you. Any high-stakes message — pricing, complaints, anything sensitive — waits here for a one-click yes or no."

> "The AI drafts. You approve. You're always in control."

This is where you handle the "is this safe?" objection before they ask it.

---

## Minute 8:15 — 9:00 — Marketing & Social (The Bonus)

**Open `/demo/marketing-social`.**

> "And on top of all that, the same system drafts your social posts, review replies, and outbound campaigns. Same rule — you approve before anything goes live."

> "So you're not just covering the phone. You're also showing up consistently on Google and Instagram without paying a marketing agency."

---

## Minute 9:00 — 10:00 — The Close

Return to `/ai-employee-system`. Look at the camera.

> "Quick recap. You'd be getting:
> - A 24/7 AI receptionist
> - A qualifier that books appointments
> - A recovery system that chases missed revenue
> - An approval layer that keeps you in control
> - And a marketing helper that keeps you visible
>
> All for less than the cost of one part-time hire."

Pause. Ask **the one question**:

> "Would you like me to set this up for [Business Name] this week?"

**Then shut up.** First one to speak loses. Let them answer.

---

## If They Say YES

- Paste the calendar link immediately
- Send `CLIENT_SETUP_FORM.md` within 10 minutes of the call
- Add them to the CRM under "Awaiting Setup Form"

## If They Say "Send Me Info"

- Send the link to `/ai-employee-system` plus a short recap email
- Pull from `SALES_OUTREACH_KIT.md` for the email template
- Book the follow-up before you hang up

## If They Say NO

Ask the one follow-up:

> "What would make this a no-brainer for you?"

Listen. That answer is your next pitch.

---

## Demo Don'ts

- Don't mention LLMs, models, agents, prompts, databases, or migrations
- Don't show internal dashboards or admin tools
- Don't quote pricing on this call unless they push hard
- Don't promise features we haven't built
- Don't go over 12 minutes — discipline matters more than completeness

---

## After Every Demo

- [ ] Log the result (Yes / Maybe / No) in the CRM
- [ ] If Yes — send setup form + calendar link
- [ ] If Maybe — schedule a follow-up call within 7 days
- [ ] If No — note the reason for next pitch improvement
- [ ] Run a 60-second self-debrief: what did they react to most?

The demo gets sharper every time. Track which page got the biggest reaction — that's where you slow down on the next one.
