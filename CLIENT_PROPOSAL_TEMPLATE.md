## Recommended solution: AI employee team

We recommend deploying a coordinated **AI employee team** with distinct roles. Each agent can be tuned to your workflows and escalations.

| Role | Function |
|------|-----------|
| **Intake Agent** | Greets prospects, captures contact details, and routes the conversation by intent (sales, support, booking). |
| **Lead Qualifier Agent** | Asks structured questions to score fit, urgency, and service match before your team invests time. |
| **Booking Agent** | Offers available times, confirms appointments via your **approved** booking link or process, and sends confirmations. |
| **Follow-Up Agent** | Sends timely reminders and nurture messages based on rules you approve (no surprise outreach). |
| **Human Handoff Agent** | Detects complex, sensitive, or high-value situations and **hands off to a person** with context summarized for your team. |
| **Missed Revenue Tracker** | Logs missed or stalled opportunities so leadership can review patterns and improve processes. |
| **Marketing / Social AI Assistant** | Drafts and schedules **human-approved** social copy, replies, and campaign ideas aligned with your brand guidelines. |

Together, these roles form a **front desk layer** that scales with demand while preserving your standards for tone, compliance, and customer experience.

## Approval and safety rules

**Human approval before sensitive actions** is a core design principle.

- **Booking confirmations**, **refunds**, **discounts beyond policy**, and **changes to legal or medical advice** require rules and, where applicable, **explicit human approval** before the AI presents them as final.  
- **Marketing and social content** is drafted for **human review** before publishing, unless you grant a narrowly defined auto-publish policy in writing.  
- **Escalation** to a live person is always available for frustrated customers, compliance-sensitive topics, or when confidence is below the threshold we configure with you.

Mercy Speaks Digital configures guardrails to match your industry and risk tolerance; the client remains responsible for business decisions and regulatory compliance.
